02
January · Thai Month

பொங்கல் Pongal

"Let it overflow." — When the harvest comes home, Tamil hearts overflow with gratitude to the Sun God, to the cattle, and to the earth itself.

🎋
🎋
🌸🌼🌸

The Festival of Harvest

Every January, as the sun begins its northward journey, Tamil Nadu awakens to the most beloved festival of the year. Pongal is not just a harvest festival — it is a profound expression of gratitude.

🌞 Celebrated during Thai Pongal — the Tamil month of Thai (Jan–Feb), marking Uttarayan — the sun's northward journey
🌾 The word "Pongal" means "to boil and overflow" — symbolising abundance and prosperity
🐄 Mattu Pongal honours the cattle — the backbone of Tamil agriculture

Four Days of Celebration

Day 1
Bhogi Pongal

The old is discarded. Bonfires are lit. A new chapter begins.

Day 2
Thai Pongal

Rice is cooked in clay pots. When it overflows — "Pongalo Pongal!" fills the air.

Day 3
Mattu Pongal

Cattle are honoured, decorated with bells and flowers — our eternal farming partners.

Day 4
Kaanum Pongal

Families reunite. Brothers pray for their sisters. The harvest celebration concludes.

"Before the harvest arrives,
hope arrives first."

April · Chithirai Month

தமிழ் புத்தாண்டு Tamil New Year

As mango blossoms fill the air and kolam patterns glow at doorsteps, Tamil Nadu welcomes a new year with blessings, feasts, and ancient rituals.

Kolam — drawn at dawn, welcoming the new year

Kani — The Auspicious Sight

The first sight of the new year morning — an arrangement of fruits, flowers, a mirror, gold jewellery, and the sacred Thiruvannamalai lamp. What you see first on Tamil New Year's Day shapes the year ahead.

🥭 Raw mango
🌼 Golden flowers
🪔 Oil lamp
📿 Gold jewels
🍚 New rice

"Life is like neem — sometimes bitter, sometimes sweet. Taste it all with a grateful heart."

04
November · Karthigai Month

கார்த்திகை தீபம் Karthigai Deepam

A million flames. One truth. The Festival of Lights that predates Deepavali — when the sacred flame atop Thiruvannamalai hill unites all of Tamil Nadu in a single moment of divine light.

🔥 The Sacred Flame

On the night of Karthigai Deepam, a massive beacon is lit atop Annamalai hill in Thiruvannamalai. It is visible for miles. The flame represents Lord Shiva as infinite light — the flame of consciousness that has no beginning and no end.

🪔 The Household Tradition

Every Tamil home arranges rows and rows of clay lamps in the courtyard and on every surface. The sight is breathtaking — as if the stars have descended to earth. Children carry lamps. Grandmothers sing hymns. The whole neighbourhood glows.

✨ Older Than Memory

Karthigai Deepam is perhaps the oldest light festival in India. References to it appear in Sangam literature — poems written 2,000+ years ago describe the lamps of Karthigai as though they were as ancient as the stars themselves.

"The lamp does not speak.
Yet it teaches us to shine."

October · Nine Nights

நவராத்திரி Navaratri

For nine nights, Tamil homes become living art galleries. The Golu — an elaborate arrangement of dolls on stepped platforms — transforms every household into a miniature universe of stories.

The Golu — Nine steps, representing the nine nights of Navaratri

Kolu Tradition

Families bring out their prized collection of clay dolls — some passed down for generations. Each set of steps (always odd-numbered) tells a story of mythology, nature, and everyday Tamil life.

Women at the Centre

Navaratri in Tamil Nadu is a festival led by women. Neighbours visit each other's Golu, exchange sundal (a savoury snack), and sing traditional songs. The festival strengthens community bonds.

Victory of Light

The tenth day — Vijayadasami — is considered the most auspicious day to begin new ventures, education, or learning a skill. Children are formally introduced to learning on this day.

06
October / November

தீபாவளி Deepavali

Darkness flees. Every home is lit. Every street alive. The festival of lights arrives — but in Tamil Nadu, it carries a uniquely intimate story: the defeat of the demon Narakasura at dawn.

🌅 The 4 AM Awakening

Unlike anywhere else in India, Tamil families wake up before dawn for Deepavali. An oil bath, new clothes, and the lighting of the first cracker before sunrise — marking the moment Lord Krishna defeated Narakasura.

🍬 The Sweet Language

Murukku, Ladoo, Halwa, Mysore Pak, Adhirasam — Tamil sweets exchanged between neighbours are acts of love as old as the festival itself.

👕 New Clothes, New Beginnings

Wearing new clothes on Deepavali morning is not optional — it's an expression of renewal, of casting off the old and welcoming the new, like the light after darkness.

🤝 Beyond Religion

In Tamil Nadu, Deepavali is celebrated by people of all faiths — a testament to the inclusive nature of Tamil culture, where festivals belong to everyone.

08
Year-Round

More Festivals
to Discover

The calendar of Tamil Nadu is a festival in itself — every month, every season brings its own celebration. Here are some hidden gems.

🕉️ January–February

Thaipusam

A festival of intense devotion to Lord Murugan. Devotees carry ornate kavadi (arched structures) as an act of penance and faith. The sight is both awe-inspiring and deeply moving.

Devotion · Kavadi · Murugan
🌊 July–August

Aadi Perukku

Celebrated on the 18th day of the Tamil month Aadi, this festival honours the rivers of Tamil Nadu — especially the Kaveri. Women offer prayers at riverbanks as the monsoon swells the rivers.

Rivers · Monsoon · Nature
🌺 March–April

Panguni Uthiram

The festival when divine unions are celebrated — the celestial wedding season. Temple chariots roll through the streets in grand processions known as Ther Thiruvizha.

Procession · Temples · Union
🥁 Village Festivals

Gramadeivam

Village deity festivals — the soul of rural Tamil culture. Each village's guardian deity is honoured with unique rituals, folk music, and drama that varies from village to village.

Village · Folk · Guardian Deity
🏮 January

Float Festival

At the Mariamman Teppakulam in Madurai, temple deities float on elaborately decorated rafts lit with thousands of lamps. The reflection on the sacred tank is a vision of pure magic.

Madurai · Float · Night Festival
🐂 January

Jallikattu

The ancient bull-taming sport of Tamil Nadu — a 2,500-year-old tradition that celebrates the bond between Tamil people and native cattle breeds, particularly the magnificent Kangeyam bull.

Heritage · Bull Taming · Pongal
